# Healing Hijamah — Website Review

A marketing/conversion review of two sites, through the lens that
**a person in pain or stress arrives to get their problem solved, not to take a course in cupping.**

- **Revision:** https://healing-hijamah.vercel.app/
- **Original:** https://www.healinghijamah.com/

---

## Bottom line

The **revision is a large, real upgrade** over the original — better trust-building, real content,
real policies, working articles. But both sites share the **same fatal conversion leak: a customer
who is ready to act can't actually book or even find out what it costs.** Everyone gets funneled into
"fill out a form and we'll be in touch." For someone trying to *organize relief*, that's a dead stop.

---

## The strategic problem (applies to both)

Someone with migraines, back pain, or stress wants three things, in this order:

1. **"Will this fix *my* problem?"** → reassurance tied to their symptom
2. **"What will it cost me?"** → price
3. **"When can I be seen?"** → a time they can lock in *now*

The revision nails #1. **Neither site answers #2 or #3.** No pricing appears anywhere on either site,
and there is no real calendar — just an inquiry form that says "we'll be in touch." You're making a
person in discomfort wait for a callback before they even know if they can afford it. That is where
you lose bookings.

---

## REVISION (healing-hijamah.vercel.app) — the good and the gaps

**What's genuinely strong (keep it):**

- **8 real, specific testimonials** — names, details (migraines, 6 children, "delivered my child"),
  spiritual tone. These read authentic, not dummy. Big trust asset.
- **3 full articles** (~1,200–1,400 words each) that *do* resolve at `/article/<slug>` and end with a
  "Schedule a Consultation" CTA. Real sales funnel content.
- Real **policies**, **privacy policy**, **FAQ**, services, and credentials (Khadijah, R.N.,
  women-only). This is a credible, finished-feeling site.

**Conversion gaps to fix (in priority order):**

1. **No pricing — anywhere.** You describe three packages (Essential Session, Wellness Support Program,
   Rest & Restore) but no price on any of them. Customers can't self-qualify, so they bounce or stall.
   *Add a price or a "from $X" range to every package.* Even "Essential Session — from $X, 60 min"
   removes the #1 hesitation.

2. **No real booking.** `/consultation` is a 5-field contact form ("we'll be in touch") — no calendar,
   no time slots, no availability, no deposit capture. *Wire in a real scheduler* (Calendly, Acuity,
   Square) so a motivated visitor can pick a slot and pay the deposit in one sitting. Right now your
   best-converting visitors hit a speed bump.

3. **Connect the problem to the path.** Your packages are organized by *product* (wet/dry/massage).
   Customers think in *problems*. Add a simple "Not sure where to start?" block:
   *Migraines/stress → Rest & Restore. Chronic condition → Wellness Support. General reset → Essential.*
   Instruct them, don't make them diagnose themselves.

4. **Policies page is missing the "what do I do about my problem" content** customers actually need
   before booking: **preparation** (eat/don't eat, what to wear, hydration), **aftercare** (activity
   limits, what to expect, bruising timeline), **what to bring**, and **rescheduling terms** (you state
   a non-refundable deposit but not what happens if they reschedule vs. cancel). This is exactly the
   "organize relief for me" content — add it.

5. **No service area / hours.** Policies mention mobile appointments and a "service area" but the area
   is never stated, and there are no hours anywhere. A customer can't tell if you serve them.

6. **Minor link/consistency nits:** the "Articles" nav item is a bare `#articles` anchor while the rest
   are clean routes; article URLs are `/article/` (singular) under an `/articles` (plural) index — both
   resolve, but keep an eye on internal links staying consistent. Article dates ("June 11, 2026") are
   fine — that's current, not placeholder.

**Dummy data:** None found on the revision. Testimonials, articles, and policies are all real content.
That's a notable strength.

## ORIGINAL (www.healinghijamah.com) — significantly weaker

If the revision is meant to replace this, **it should.** The original is thin on almost every axis:

- **Only one bookable thing: the free "Complimentary Assessment Call."** No actual treatment booking,
  no pricing, no session menu. The entire site funnels to a free call and stops.
- **About page is placeholder-thin** — name + one headshot + a generic sentence. No story, no
  hijama-specific training, no "why me." No trust built.
- **Contact page is broken/incomplete:** form has no message field (customer can't say why they're
  reaching out), no address, no hours, no map, and a "Stay Connected" heading with **no social links
  under it** — an empty section.
- **Refund policy is a single sentence.** No testimonials, no articles, no FAQ — almost no
  trust-building anywhere.

**Two consistency issues to reconcile between the sites:**

- **Email differs:** original uses `Deej@healinghijamah.com`; revision uses `info@healinghijamah.com`.
  Pick one.
- **Name/credentials differ:** original says "Khadijah Massop, RN, **IBCLC**"; revision says
  "Khadijah, R.N." The original's IBCLC (lactation) credential and full surname are missing from the
  revision — decide whether IBCLC is part of this brand or deliberately dropped.

## What I'd do this week (ranked)

1. **Put prices on the revision's three packages.** Single biggest leak.
2. **Replace the consultation form with a real booking calendar + deposit.** Let people organize their
   own relief instead of waiting for a callback.
3. **Add a "Where do I start?" problem→package guide.**
4. **Add prep + aftercare + reschedule terms to the policies page.**
5. **Add service area + hours.**
6. **Decommission/redirect the original** so the weaker About/Contact/refund pages don't surface in
   search and undercut the better site. Reconcile the email and name/credential differences first.

The revision has done the hard part — credibility and content. The last mile is purely **letting a
ready customer pay and book without talking to a human first.** Close that and the site goes from
"informative brochure" to "books appointments while you sleep."
